I am a Research Associate in the Department of Electrical and Software Engineering at the University of Calgary, where I contribute to microgrid research under the supervision of Prof. Andrew M. Knight. My research focuses on the control of power electronic converters, distributed energy resources, and electromechanical energy conversion.

I hold a Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from the Federal University of Santa Maria (Brazil), where I developed advanced control strategies for switched reluctance motors. I have over 10 years of engineering experience spanning academic research and industry, including postdoctoral work at the University of Calgary and McMaster University's Automotive Resource Centre (MARC) in collaboration with industrial partners.

## Research Interests
- Control of power electronic converters and electric drives
- Switched reluctance machines and synchronous reluctance machines
- Microgrids and distributed energy resources
- Sliding mode control, optimization, and sensorless control strategies

## Recent Highlights
- Contributing to the construction and operation of a campus microgrid integrating solar panels, microinverters, a flow battery, and a propane generator
- Over 25 publications including 7 top-tier IEEE journal papers (h-index: 11)
- Sessional Instructor for ENEL 582 (Modelling and Control of Electric Machines) and ENEL 585 (Introduction to Power Electronics) at the University of Calgary
- Eligible for registration as a Professional Engineer with APEGA
