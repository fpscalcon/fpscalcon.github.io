## Overview
Switched reluctance motors (SRMs) are robust and low-cost machines with significant potential 
for electric traction and renewable energy applications. My research has focused on developing 
advanced control strategies to overcome their inherent challenges, including torque ripple, 
acoustic noise, and the need for precise rotor position information.

## Key Contributions
- Developed a discrete-time super-twisting sliding mode current controller with fixed switching 
frequency, published in IEEE Transactions on Power Electronics
- Proposed sensorless control strategies for wide-speed range operation
- Investigated DC-link capacitor current reduction techniques through torque sharing function 
optimization
- Applied particle swarm optimization (PSO) for performance improvement and parameter estimation
- Developed robust control strategies using linear matrix inequalities (LMIs)

## Impact
This research resulted in over 10 publications in top-tier IEEE journals and conferences, 
including IEEE Transactions on Power Electronics, IEEE Transactions on Industrial Electronics, 
and IEEE Access. The work was conducted across three institutions: Federal University of Santa 
Maria, McMaster University, and the University of Calgary.
